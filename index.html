Welcome! My name is Austin Kurtti and I currently live in Lawrence, Kansas as an undergraduate student at the University of Kansas. I am pursuing a major in Computer Science and a minor in Business with the intent to graduate in the spring of 2017.

Before coming to KU, I was inspired to become an electrical engineer through participation in my high school's engineering club. After the first semester of classes my freshman year, I wasn't as excited to continue with engineering as I thought I would be. But with the help of my first programming class in my second semester, I had discovered what I really wanted to study. Within the first couple weeks of that class, I found myself wanting more homework and I could tell I was once again excited to stay in the engineering program. I had always viewed programming as a difficult foreign language that didn't make any sense, until I realized it's something I love. Before I knew it, I had submitted my request to change majors from electrical engineering to computer science.

In February of 2014, I started a job with AllofE Solutions in Lawrence, Kansas as a web development intern. After working at AllofE for over a year, I have learned that web design is what I truly enjoy the most. With two years left in my academic career, I look forward to continuing my studies at KU and learning more about the web development industry through my internship with AllofE.

SmartPath
Mobile Application
Harkurtis Mobile Systems
Overview:
SmartPath is a mobile application with the intent to improve student safety on campus. It's most prominent service, NightWalk, is designed to be a quicker alternative to blue light poles, by immediately and reliably connecting the student to emergency services. Currently, there are three services planned to be bundled into SmartPath: NightWalk, SafeRide, and SOS. NightWalk will be the first service implemented and will be the only one available during the app's first beta test. SafeRide and SOS will be implemented soon after. If you would like more information, please visit the Harkurtis website!
Shape Attack
Mobile Application
Personal Project
Shape Attack is a personal project I work on during my spare time. The easiest way to describe it would be a rough mix between Bubble Breaker and Tetris. The player controls a shape gun at the bottom of the screen, which they use to shoot shapes at falling bricks. These bricks contain outlines of different shapes the player can fill. Some bricks have one outline while others have three or more. Bricks fall faster if they have fewer outlines. The player's objective is to shoot corresponding shapes at a brick to fill in its outlines. Once a brick has all of its outlines filled in, it disappears and the player receives a point reward. If a brick manages to fall to the bottom of the screen, the player will be penalized one life point. Some bricks will contain power-ups like slower brick speeds, frozen time, and one-shape-fits-all. Upon breaking these bricks, the player will be rewarded with the power-up. As the player progresses through levels, the game will become increasingly difficult, with more outlines per brick, faster brick speeds, and fewer power-ups. The player's total points will become their final score when they lose all three lives.

I am still in the early development phases of this game, but hope for it to be complete by the end of 2015 or early 2016. Since I am currently building it with native Android libraries, it will available first on the Google Play Store.
